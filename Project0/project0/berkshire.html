The original Berkshire pig was reddish or sandy-brown, sometimes with spots. In the 1700s, breeders made improvements by cross-breeding the Berkshire with a Chinese and Siamese pig. This is when the breed took on the distinctive entirely black color, with white on the feet, nose, and tail. Go to Wikipedia to continue reading

The Berkshire has a long heritage - it's the oldest recorded breed in Britain! Producing litter sizes of an average of 10 piglets, the breed is known for its good maternal instincts and docile nature. -Countryfile
